What is a Black Hole?

What is a Black Hole?

A black hole is a black object that can come in a range of sizes and pulls things in that get too close. There are black holes at the center of most galaxies.

Time Dilation
Time Dilation

Time Dilation

When an object gets close enough to a black hole, it experiences time dilation. Time dilation happens near a black hole where time slows down for something falling into a black hole. The time will seem normal to the thing (or person) falling in but to an outside observer it would look like whatever’s falling in is slowing down and getting red shifted.

Event Horizon
Event Horizon

Event Horizon

The event horizon is the point of no return where if something passes the event horizon, it cannot escape the black hole’s gravity.

Gravitational Pull
Gravitational Pull

Gravitational Pull

The black hole has a strong gravitational field that pulls things close to it.

Spaghettification
Spaghettification

Spaghettification

If someone is falling into a black hole, they would get stretched out from the part closest to the black hole.

Accretion Disk
Accretion Disk

Accretion Disk

The accretion disk is a disk that stretches around the black hole, looks warped around the black hole, and is made of light falling in the black hole.

Singularity
Singularity

Singularity

The singularity is an infinitely small point where everything gets pulled into and has infinite gravity.
